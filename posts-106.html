A Japanese Zen Garden in Calgary Seems to Be Unusual, Right?

By Jose G. Monagas, March 2024

Summer 2024 is finally here and we couldn't be happier — although we know that everyone will be on the go with competing priorities. Unsurprisingly, it will be challenging to find moments of peace and tranquility.

So, if you are one of those busy Calgarians who is racing from one task to another, with little time to relax and recharge, then Japanese Zen style gardening and landscaping is the perfect solution for you.

Now, you might be thinking: Creating a Japanese and Zen style garden or landscape in Calgary seems like an unusual choice, considering the harsh climate and limited space. And, Yes, you might also be wondering: How an ancient art form that allows for creativity and self-expression can benefit a modern and busy Calgarian.

The good news is that Japanese Zen style gardening and landscaping can bring balance and calmness into your hectic life, and only requires you to be fully present in the moment as a means to reduce stress and anxiety, allowing you to find a sense of calm and balance.

As a minimalist style of gardening, its purpose is to create a peaceful and harmonious environment that promotes mindfulness and inner peace by using carefully arranged rocks, gravel, and shrubs. Then, as you gently rake the gravel and arrange the rocks in your garden, it helps to clear your mind and focus on the present.

Consequently, by having a Zen style garden in your backyard — or even a small corner of your balcony or bedroom — you can escape the noise and find moments of tranquility amidst the turmoil.

Unlike traditional lawns and flower bed gardens that require constant watering, pruning, and weeding, Zen style gardens need only occasionally raking and cleaning. This makes them an ideal choice for those with a busy schedule — as it does not require much time or effort to maintain.

You can create your own unique design, with the placement of rocks and gravel representing different elements such as mountains, rivers, or islands. Next, you could add some plants, such as bonsai trees or moss — this can provide a sense of accomplishment and further enhance the feeling of being in a natural environment.

The question now is: Why would you not give it a try and create your own little Zen style garden in 2024? Calgary continues to grow and evolve, and so does its residents. While this unique and tranquil style of landscaping has been around for centuries, Japanese and Zen style gardening and landscaping is the one trend that has been gaining popularity in recent years, and is now making its way to private homes in Calgary — it is expected to become even more popular in the coming years.
