Creating a Japanese Style Garden on a Budget in Calgary

By Jose G. Monagas, December 2023

As the average house price in Calgary is expected to be $564,000 at the end of 2023, a Japanese style landscape can significantly boost your property's value.

You may need to hire a landscape architect to help you design your Japanese garden, choose the right plants, layout, and structures. The cost for this service can range from $2,000 to $6,000.

Next, you will need a garden design plan to guide the landscaping crews during the construction phase. The cost for this can range from $2,000 to $6,000.

The choice of plants and trees is a crucial aspect of a Japanese style garden. Depending on the species and quantity you choose, this could cost you between $5,000 and $30,000. We recommend you consult with a local nursery or horticultural expert when planning, as Calgary's climate may not be suitable for all species.

Your shortlist of plants could include: 10 species of lilacs (for their fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring); 10 species of maples (for their vibrant fall colours); 10 species of bushes (for their hardiness and suitability for gardens in Calgary); 10 species of pines (for their year round greenery); and 10 species of caraganas (for their ability to survive in harsh climates).

Rocks and gravel are essential elements of Japanese gardens. These could cost you from $2,000 to $6,000. Adding fixed structures such as stone lanterns, entrance gates, and fences can add to the authenticity of your Japanese style garden. These structures could cost between $5,000 and $20,000. The cost of transporting and delivering materials to your home can range from $2,000 to $6,000.

The labour costs for landscaping crews can vary greatly, depending on the complexity of the design and the size of your garden. These costs could range from $10,000 to $50,000. Maintaining a Japanese garden might be challenging for you alone. Thus, you may need to budget between $1,000 and $6,000 for yearly maintenance and upkeep.

There Are Several Other Factors to Consider When Budgeting for Your Japanese Garden

Depending on the scale of your project and local regulations, you may need to obtain City permits. The cost of these permits can vary. If your garden design includes water features or outdoor lighting, there will be utility costs to consider, unless you use solar powered features.

As some plants may not survive the winter, you may need to budget for replacing plants or providing them with winter protection. Pests can be a problem in any garden, and you may need to budget for pest control measures.

If you are not a gardening expert, you might need to hire a professional for regular maintenance tasks like pruning, shaping, weeding, etc. Depending on the value of your garden, especially with expensive structures or rare plants, you might want to consider getting them insured. With careful planning and budgeting, you can create a serene and elegant Japanese style landscape. If you need help, contact Ishidoro Zen Gardens Inc.
