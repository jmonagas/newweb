Key Elements of Japanese Style Gardens

The key elements of Japanese Gardens include: Plants, Trees, Shrubs, Hedges, Moss, Grass, Rocks, Stones, Boulders, Water, Ponds, Waterfalls, Pools, Streams, Basins, Sand, Gravel, Paths, Bridges, Lanterns, Pergolas, Fences, Gates, Verandas, and more. The five elements of Zen are earth, fire, water, wood and metal. They work together to balance the energy in your environment. Our gardens and landscapes showcase the mainstream characteristics of Zen and Japanese style gardens and landscapes, with a focus on local sceneries, plants and materials that are environmentally sustainable and non invasive.

In our experience of over a decade of continuous search and learning, we have found that Zen dry gardens are no less than complex spaces with an all encompassing meaning that is as thought provoking and timeless as the pyramids for the ancient Egyptians. You will find in your own exploration journey that the concept of monotone (or limited color palette) in the context of Zen contributes to enhancing a garden's tranquility and aesthetic appeal.

List of Suggested Landscaping Themes

The list below is non exhaustive, and only serves the purpose of suggesting a few types of gardens and landscapes that our clients could order. Ishidoro Zen Gardens firmly believes there is beauty in austerity. We consciously choose to follow the principles of the craft while making the necessary adjustments to suit the climate and ecology of our region. Our work is a labor of love, with a deep appreciation of the Japanese aesthetics and the cultural values of simplicity and harmony with nature.
