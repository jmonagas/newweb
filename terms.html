Standard Operating Procedure

At Ishidoro Zen Gardens, our primary objective is to optimize business operations by ensuring efficiency, maintaining work quality, promoting uniformity, and achieving top performance. We prioritize minimizing miscommunication and non-compliance with regulations. To facilitate this, we have developed a straightforward, step-by-step workflow that is easy to comprehend and follow. The list below provides a summary of our standardized operating procedure.

  1. After you contact Ishidoro Zen Gardens to arrange your free consultation, our team will promptly gather information regarding your specific preferences and lifestyle.
  2. You and our team will choose a date and time to visit the site and get a clear idea of the available space for your project. Feel free to ask questions and express any concerns.
  3. Next, our team will focus on developing practical diagrams and conceptual design blueprints for your personalized Japanese style gardens and landscapes.
  4. You and our team will have another meeting to go over the plans and drawings, while we gather more information and hear your feedback on any specific expectations.
  5. Once more, you and our team will meet to conduct a final assessment of the design, contract, warranties, payment arrangements, and official approval to move forward.
  6. Ishidoro Zen Gardens will provide you with estimated start and end dates; and very soon after that, your garden project will move into the production phase.
  7. From the commencement date, you will have unlimited access to all communication channels until the very end, when you do the final walkthrough and project inspection.
  8. After your final inspection and approval, we will hand you the closing invoice and receive the balance payment. Thank you for referring Ishidoro Zen Gardens to your friends, and sharing a Google review.
  9. You will receive a free estimate for regular maintenance and care, without any obligations. This way, you can be sure that your gardens and landscapes will always be in their best condition.
